Liquid Death

How Liquid Death's Founder Started A $700 Million Water Brand | Founder Effect

Learnings:

  • Make branding that stands out - but in an extreme way!
  • Make something boring look super exciting and interesting!
  • Orientate to your customers - “What is a pain point in their life?” or at least “What is something essential that is actually still super boring right now?” → Idee: Toilettenpapier (wie z. B. Dude Wipes)
    • Find a niche (of consumer goods) with boring products only and launch a fancy and interesting product with a strong marketing campaign within that niche.
  • The product itself (taste, smell, etc.) doesn’t have to be the best on the market - it’s more about the point: “Can you make something so interesting someone is gonna pick it up (e. g. in the supermarket)?”
  • First create an MVP e. g. a realistic mockup and start advertising this. When you get a lot of interest, use that to convince investors and other important strategic partners!

General:

  • (Mountain) water in a can with funny/ satirical branding and look!

  • Mostly advertised and sold in the US
I love Virgin's sort of business strategy, which was find a really stale category of products and be the one really cool, exciting product in it. And they were able to get all this market share really easily because they were just so disruptive and sticking out like a sore thumb in whatever kind of category that was.
The only way the brand would have a chance at survival is the actual product itself has to be so insanely interesting where so much of the marketing is baked into the product where if someone sees this on the shelf, am I willing to bet they have to pick it up because it's so weird or interesting and then they're probably going to take their phone out, take a photo of it and post it on their social channels for free to their hundreds of followers.
  • To prove an idea is viable, make some piece of content for it like a commercial (MVP) (in this case the can does not have to exist but has to be rendered in a realistic 3D model), use some marketing budget to spread it on the internet, take this feedback (comments, views, likes, etc.) of a potentially viral character and use that to get investors on board to give you more expertise and especially money!
People think that taste is why things are successful or unsuccessful, or why one brand is better than another. All the data shows that is not even close to the case. [Branding is!] Monster didn't become a $50 billion company because it tastes so much better than Red Bull. Most people probably couldn't pick out an energy drink in a blind taste test if their life depended on. Our brand is all about comedy and making people laugh. We're not tying our brand to some specific niche like action sports. So I think as long as we're constantly just riffing on culture, just can go basically for as long as something like Saturday Night Live is relevant because you're literally just a part of culture and making people laugh.
