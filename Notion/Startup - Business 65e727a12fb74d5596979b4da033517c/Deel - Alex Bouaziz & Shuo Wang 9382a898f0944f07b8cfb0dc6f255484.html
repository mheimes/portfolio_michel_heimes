Deel - Alex Bouaziz & Shuo Wang

  • Founded deel, which is a company that does all of the back office for companies that want to hire globally
    • Deel takes care of all the different local regulations and requirements/ makes sure that it is easy and not completely overwhelming for any business to hire people from around the world.

Main takeaways:

  • You have to iterate a lot. (more here)
  • It’s all about market validation and product validation. You don’t need 100,000 customers, you need the first 100 real users. (more here)
  • The right point to hiring is when you think you can start scaling the cell cycle of your business. (more here)
  • Why limit to a small (local) radius for finding and hiring people and letting them work in a physical office, when you could get the best talent worldwide to work for you remotely. (more here)
  • If you want to scale your business globally, you should set up small sales teams in many regions worldwide, even if it just one person each. That helps you build the infrastructure and fundamentals to run your business. (more here)
  • Have your own company principles that represents what matters to you as a company and which reflect the values you represent towards customers and other internal people. (more here)
    • Example: Don’t push exercises back if not absolutely necessary but do them right away and with greatest customer care. (more here) → “If you were the customer, what’d you want from us? Do exactly this.”
  • Trust in the people who work with/ for you is essential to be able to scale a business BUT should be combined with strong KPIs. What’s important is that everyone knows what goals and vision they are working towards, so that they don’t get lost on the way and feel like their work doesn’t really have an impact. (more here)
  • Work should be fun and empowering. This can only be the state when you’re healthy. This includes physical, mental and emotional health. All three are similarly important and should in no case be underestimated. Carrying any type of a burden is contra-productive in almost every case you can think of, especially communication, social relationships and solution finding. With at least one of these three health types being unbalanced the smallest issue can already cause great stress and madness. (more here)
  • If you have an idea for something in your life that you want to pursue, spend a couple of hours on it every day, whenever possible, and see what happens. Even the smallest impulse of action from your side can bring you forward. (more here)
  • What makes certain companies or geographical areas so successful and economically important is not only their infrastructure but their mentality to do their thing, no matter what. No matter what others think and no matter who will care if they fail (even though this is most likely no one really). (more here)
  • If you want to be a music producer, do it. If you want to be an influencer, do it. If you want to build a business, do it. You only live once, so just do whatever makes you happy - but be serious about it and give it your all. (more here)

Advices/ Quotes:

  • Change perspectives: Deel began to have success once they changed from targeting freelancers to use their product to get paid, to targeting the companies that hired the freelancers to use their product to hire/ pay/ terminate freelancers/ contracts/ etc..
