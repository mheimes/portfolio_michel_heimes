Aufbau eines Pitches

Orientiert an dieser Webseite!

A pitch deck is usually a 10-15 slide presentation deck designed to give a short summary of your company, your business plan and your startup vision.

A pitch deck presentation serves different purposes, from trying to get a meeting with a new investor, to presenting in front of a stage, and each one of them follows a different structure!

  1. Cover
    • Very basic: company and tagline (5-7 words that summarize the core mission of the company - appropriate jargon/ language)
    • Tagline example (Airbnb): “Book rooms with locals, rather than hotels”
  1. Problem slide
    • Describe exactly the problems (the three greatest problems) for which you will be offering solutions in simple words and in a way that allows your listeners to empathize with the problems easily.
    • Highlight the most important keyword/ keyphrase in each problem sentence that should stick.
    • Problems example (Airbnb):
      • “Price is an important concern for customers booking travel online.”
      • “Hotels leave you disconnected from the city and its culture.”
      • “No easy way exists to book a room with a local or become a host.”
  1. Solution slide
    • Describe the product in a simple sentence.
    • Summarize the three most important reasons why a customer would buy your product → core value proposition.
    • Talk about the benefits rather than the features and summarize the benefits so that they can be seen as a great solution to the problem by the majority and not only by a niche.
    • Solutions example (Airbnb):
      • Product description: “A web platform where users can rent out their space to travelers to “…
      • … “Save Money when traveling”
      • … “Make Money when hosting”
      • … “Share Culture local connection to the city”
  1. Market validation
    • Comparison to the user numbers of comparable companies
    • Provide the closest existing reference to a company in your space, to give investors a real idea of your market opportunity.
    • Example:
      • “craigslist.com 670,000 TOTAL USERS”
      • couchsurfing.com 17,000 TEMPORARY HOUSING LISTINGS In SF & NYC from 7/09 to 7/16”
  1. Market size
    • TAM/ SAM/ SOM
    • Show the size of your opportunity - size of industry and its expected behavior for the upcoming years.
    • Confirm credible 3rd party proof for the data you provide.
  1. Product
    • Summarize core functionality of the product in as few words/ images as possible.
    • Another great way to approach this this slide is with a short 30 second video. A video is truly worth a thousand images. That’s definitely the way you should go. → Make sure to keep the images version around just in case sound, internet, etc. fails.

      • Search by city → Review listings → Book it!

  1. Business Model
    • Summarize your business model in a single line like: → “We take 10% commission on each transaction.”
    • Describe your revenue streams by simplifying them with example calculations with average numbers.
    • Reference to previous slides, especially the slide on market size.
    • Can be a killer slide (in a positive way) if well structured. Can show/ define:
      • simple business model
      • (huge) potential the company has (in an existing market)
      • the future vision where the company expect the product to be in x years time
  1. Adoption Strategy/ Market Adoption
    • Summarize 2-3 core customer acquisition hacks that you will use to grow the business.
      • What methods are better, more scalable and more profitable than your competition? → Show them here!
  1. Competition
    • A 2-axis diagram is the perfect way to establish against your competition.
    • Define two USPs (the two core differentiators) of your product as the horizontal and the vertical axis.
      • Easy way to visually convey your unique selling points.
      • Make sure to setup the chart so that your product shows up in the top right corner of the chart.
  1. Competitive Advantages
    • Goal if the slide: Answer questions before they come up!
    • Point out the core benefits (, not features!) that will allow you to outrun your competitors.
  1. Team
    • Stick to the founding team for this slide
      • The “Hustler/ Hacker/ Hipster” trio is often an ideal combination here.
      • Avoid adding advisors on this slide as they are usually not relevant enough to make the cut for your pitch deck.
      • Focus particularly on listing each founder’s entrepreneurial achievements, rather than their academic titles.
        • In the end, experience matters a LOT more than academic titles.
  1. Press
    • Provide validation a credibility
      • Goals of the press slide:
        1. Prove that your product/ concept is interesting enough to be featured in ___.
        1. Proves that your team was clever enough to get you featured.
    • For the user testimonials, make sure to pick reviews that talk about the core benefits of your product, as a confirmation that your customers are understanding the underlying value.
  1. User testimonials
    • For the user testimonials, make sure to pick reviews that talk about the core benefits of your product, as a confirmation that your customers are understanding the underlying value.
  1. Financial
    • Only relevant if you are actually looking for funds → the “Ask” slide
      • This slide includes the details of your round or fundraising efforts.
      • Be careful with the information that you disclose here, general solicitation legislation prohibits the disclosure of some information, that might jeopardize your company and even your investors in the future.
        • Have at least two versions of your pitch deck. One deck with no financials, that you can share around to get intros and meetings, and another deck with disclosed details of your company’s financials and projections for meetings once you established link with your investor(s).
Aufbau eines auf das nötigste reduzierte Pitch decks

Struktur entsprechend nach Guy Kawasaki

3 main rules:

  1. A pitch should consist of 10 slides & no more than 15.
  1. The presentation should last no more than 20 minutes.
  1. The font to it should be smaller than 30 points.

Structure:

  1. Cover
  1. Problem (/ Opportunity)
  1. Solution (/ Value Proposition)
  1. Underlying Magic (describe/ “brag about” your product or service)
  1. Business Model
  1. Adoption Strategy (/ Go-to-Market Plan)
  1. Competition
  1. Team
  1. Financial (Projections and Key Metrics)
  1. Current status, accomplishments to date, timeline and use of funds
